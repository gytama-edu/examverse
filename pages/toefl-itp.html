ITP
TOEFL ITP overview

Institutional testing with a strong grammar core.

TOEFL ITP is commonly used by institutions for placement, progress monitoring, and academic English proficiency purposes.

Unlike TOEFL iBT, the classic TOEFL ITP preparation path focuses strongly on listening, grammar, written expression, and reading. It is a different test with a different task structure.

i Exam Verse focuses on learning and practice. It does not save student progress.

Avoid predictable mistakes

Common TOEFL ITP Traps

Knowing the trap makes it easier to slow down and choose with evidence.

TRAP 01

Choosing an answer because it sounds familiar

Why it traps you: It repeats words but changes the meaning.

Student tip: Match the complete idea, not one familiar word.

TRAP 02

Ignoring the real subject and verb

Why it traps you: Extra phrases hide the sentence core.

Student tip: Find the main subject and verb before checking details.

TRAP 03

Missing negative expressions

Why it traps you: A small negative word can reverse the meaning.

Student tip: Mark words such as not, hardly, and except.

TRAP 04

Confusing similar-sounding words

Why it traps you: Sound similarity distracts from the message.

Student tip: Use sentence context to confirm what you heard.

TRAP 05

Overlooking prepositions

Why it traps you: A familiar phrase may need one exact preposition.

Student tip: Learn prepositions as part of complete expressions.

TRAP 06

Reading only one sentence instead of the full context

Why it traps you: Nearby ideas may change or clarify the answer.

Student tip: Read before and after the target line.

Use scores as a study map

TOEFL ITP Score Guide

A score is most useful when it points you toward the skill that needs attention next.

TOEFL ITP Level 1 and Level 2 scores are based on correct answers converted into scaled scores.

Students usually receive section scores for Listening Comprehension, Structure and Written Expression, and Reading Comprehension.

Use your result to identify weak areas—not just to chase a number. Exam Verse focuses on practice and learning, not official scoring.

Smart study strategy

How to Study TOEFL ITP Smarter

Build accuracy first, then add speed and test pressure gradually.

01

Master grammar patterns first

Learn to see the sentence core before solving more complex structures.

02

Review every wrong answer

Write down the rule or clue that would have led you to the answer.

03

Train your ear for restatement

Practice recognizing the same idea expressed with different words.

04

Read questions before scanning

Know what information you need before searching the passage.

05

Build academic vocabulary

Learn words in context with common forms, partners, and meanings.

06

Practice under time pressure

Use short timed sets after your method becomes accurate and reliable.

ITP vs iBT

TOEFL ITP and TOEFL iBT are different tests. TOEFL ITP is commonly used by institutions for placement, progress monitoring, and internal academic English purposes, while TOEFL iBT is widely used for international admissions.

Digital program note
