IELTS Academic overview

Learn the English—and how each task works.

IELTS Academic is commonly used for university admission, professional registration, and academic purposes. Strong preparation combines language development with clear task strategy.

Listening and Speaking are shared with General Training. Academic Reading uses longer academic-style texts, while Academic Writing has its own report and essay requirements.

i Exam Verse focuses on learning and practice. It does not save student progress.

Understand your result

IELTS Academic Band Score Guide

Use the band scale to identify your next practice target—not to label your ability.

IELTS uses band scores from 0 to 9. Students receive a score for Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking.

The overall band score is based on the four skill scores. Writing and Speaking are assessed using criteria, not only correct answers.

Use your band score to identify weak areas and plan focused practice.

4–4.5Building foundation
5–5.5Developing user
6–6.5Competent academic user
7–7.5Strong academic user
8–9Very strong to expert user
Avoid predictable mistakes

Common IELTS Academic Traps

Recognize the habit behind the mistake, then replace it with a clear check.

TRAP 01

Describing every number in Task 1

Why it traps you: The report becomes a list without priorities.

Student tip: Select the most important features and group related data.

TRAP 02

Writing without a clear overview

Why it traps you: The reader cannot see the main pattern.

Student tip: State the biggest trends or differences in a separate overview.

TRAP 03

Giving memorized answers in Speaking

Why it traps you: Prepared language may sound unnatural or miss the question.

Student tip: Answer directly, then develop a real idea or experience.

TRAP 04

Confusing False with Not Given

Why it traps you: Missing information is not the same as opposing information.

Student tip: Choose False only when the text clearly contradicts the statement.

TRAP 05

Ignoring essay task words

Why it traps you: A fluent essay can still answer the wrong question.

Student tip: Underline the topic, instruction, and required parts before planning.

TRAP 06

Using informal language in academic writing

Why it traps you: Casual wording weakens the expected academic tone.

Student tip: Use precise, neutral language without forcing complicated words.

Smart study strategy

How to Study IELTS Academic Smarter

Turn broad practice into small habits you can review and repeat.

01

Learn task types first

Know what every question asks before adding time pressure.

02

Build a Task 1 overview habit

Find and state the biggest patterns before describing detail.

03

Practice essays by question type

Use a reliable planning method for each kind of Task 2 prompt.

04

Read for evidence, not opinion

Base every answer on what the passage states or logically shows.

05

Record speaking answers

Listen for clarity, repetition, hesitation, and room to develop ideas.

06

Review mistakes with band criteria

Connect feedback to the specific skill or assessment criterion involved.
