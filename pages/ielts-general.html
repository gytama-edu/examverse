IELTS General Training overview

Build English for work, migration, and daily life.

IELTS General Training is commonly used for migration, work, training, and general English purposes. Students need to understand everyday English, workplace communication, and clear written responses.

Listening and Speaking are shared with Academic. General Reading and General Writing are different from Academic and require the correct preparation path.

i Exam Verse focuses on learning and practice. It does not save student progress.

Understand your result

IELTS General Training Band Score Guide

Use the band scale to identify your next practice target—not to label your ability.

IELTS uses band scores from 0 to 9. Students receive a score for Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking.

The overall band score is based on the four skill scores. Writing and Speaking are assessed using criteria, not only correct answers.

Use your band score to identify weak areas and plan focused practice.

4–4.5Building foundation
5–5.5Developing user
6–6.5Competent user
7–7.5Strong user
8–9Very strong to expert user
Avoid predictable mistakes

Common IELTS General Training Traps

Recognize the habit behind the mistake, then replace it with a practical check.

TRAP 01

Writing a letter with the wrong tone

Why it traps you: The language does not fit your relationship with the reader.

Student tip: Identify who the reader is before choosing openings and requests.

TRAP 02

Forgetting one bullet point in Task 1

Why it traps you: The response does not fully cover the task.

Student tip: Turn each bullet point into a clear part of your plan.

TRAP 03

Writing too much for Task 1 and losing Task 2 time

Why it traps you: Extra detail takes time from the longer essay.

Student tip: Set a firm Task 1 time limit and move on.

TRAP 04

Confusing False with Not Given

Why it traps you: Missing information is not the same as opposing information.

Student tip: Choose False only when the text clearly contradicts the statement.

TRAP 05

Ignoring workplace vocabulary in Reading

Why it traps you: Key instructions and requirements may use unfamiliar terms.

Student tip: Learn workplace words inside real notices and documents.

TRAP 06

Giving memorized answers in Speaking

Why it traps you: Prepared language may sound unnatural or miss the question.

Student tip: Answer directly, then add a genuine reason or example.

Smart study strategy

How to Study IELTS General Training Smarter

Turn practical language into small habits you can review and repeat.

01

Learn letter tone first

Match vocabulary, greetings, and requests to the reader and relationship.

02

Practice every bullet point

Train yourself to cover each required purpose with useful detail.

03

Build workplace vocabulary

Learn common words through notices, policies, schedules, and job documents.

04

Read for purpose and detail

Ask why a text exists before locating the exact information you need.

05

Practice essays by question type

Use a reliable planning method for each kind of Task 2 prompt.

06

Record speaking answers

Listen for clarity, repetition, hesitation, and room to develop ideas.
