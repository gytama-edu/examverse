IELTS General Training overview

Build English for work, migration, and daily life.

IELTS General Training is commonly used for migration, work, training, and general English purposes. Students need to understand everyday English, workplace communication, and clear written responses.

Listening and Speaking are shared with Academic. General Reading and General Writing are different from Academic and require the correct preparation path.

i Exam Verse focuses on learning and practice. It does not save student progress.

Listening Practice

Follow information from social situations into training discussions and academic-style talks.

PART 01

Everyday Conversation

Practice understanding basic information in a social conversation.

Student focus

  • Names
  • Numbers
  • Dates
  • Times
  • Places
  • Forms
  • Specific details
PART 02

Everyday Monologue

Practice listening to one speaker giving information about a general situation.

Student focus

  • Main points
  • Directions
  • Facilities
  • Instructions

PART 03

Educational or Training Discussion

Practice listening to two or more speakers discussing study, training, or planning topics.

Student focus

  • Opinions
  • Agreement and disagreement
  • Speaker attitude
  • Choices
  • Reasons and examples
PART 04

Academic-Style Talk

Practice listening to one speaker explaining an academic or training-related subject.

Student focus

  • Main idea
  • Supporting details
  • Classification
  • Cause and effect
  • Examples
  • Note completion

GENERAL WRITING CORE
W1
Skill 03 · Task 1

Writing Task 1: Letter Writing

Choose the right tone for the relationship, then cover every purpose clearly.

LETTER 01

Informal Letter

Write to someone you know well, such as a friend or family member.

Student focus

  • Friendly tone
  • Natural opening
  • Personal details
  • Clear reason
  • Suitable closing
LETTER 02

Semi-formal Letter

Write to someone you know but not closely, such as a teacher, colleague, landlord, or neighbour.

Student focus

  • Polite tone
  • Clear situation
  • Helpful details
  • Balanced friendliness
  • Appropriate closing
LETTER 03

Formal Letter

Write to someone you do not know personally, such as a manager, company, institution, or public office.

Student focus

  • Formal tone
  • Clear purpose
  • Direct request
  • Organized details
  • Professional closing
Common Letter Purposes
  • Requesting information
  • Explaining a problem
  • Making a complaint
  • Giving advice
  • Apologizing
  • Inviting
  • Thanking
  • Suggesting a solution

Skill 04

Speaking Practice

Move from familiar questions to a long turn and deeper discussion.

PART 01

Introduction and Interview

Answer familiar questions about yourself, your daily life, interests, studies, work, and opinions.

Student focus

  • Natural answers
  • Short explanation
  • Everyday vocabulary
  • Fluency
PART 02

Cue Card / Long Turn

Speak for a longer time about a given topic using notes and clear organization.

Student focus

  • Story structure
  • Details
  • Past events
  • Description
  • Personal experience
PART 03

Discussion

Give deeper opinions, compare ideas, discuss causes, effects, and future possibilities.

Student focus

  • Abstract ideas
  • Opinion development
  • Examples
  • Comparing views
  • Speculation
